## CHANGELOG

### v0 — The Origin

I started as a system engineer — three years of learning how large systems hold together and how they fall apart. But something kept pulling me toward the screen. The craft of building what people actually see and touch. So I quit my job, moved halfway across the world, and bet everything on starting over. Learned the messy way. Built things before I fully understood them. That habit never left.

### v1 — The Grind

Then it got serious. Brand sites, enterprise dashboards, SaaS platforms with layers of business logic that'd keep a whiteboard busy for days. I got good at the unsexy stuff: clean architecture, reliable delivery, keeping every promise I made. I became the name that kept coming up when something needed to ship right. Somewhere in there I stopped being a developer. I became the engineer people trust to make things work.

### v2 — The Turn

React and TypeScript sharpened how I build. AI changed what I build. I went from tinkering with LLMs to orchestrating multiple models, then designing full agentic systems with RAG, MCP tooling, and tool-calling patterns. Not side projects. Production systems. I didn't abandon the frontend. I just gave it a brain.

### latest — The Now

I flex between tech lead and solo operator depending on what's needed. I architect, mentor, unblock. But the thing people notice first isn't my code — it's that I communicate clearly, show up without ego, and keep the energy right even when timelines shift and requirements mutate. I rebuilt my career from scratch once. Reinventing what I build is just what I do.
